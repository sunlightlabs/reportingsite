Reports of possible mortgage fraud

The map below charts suspicious activity reports, or SARs, relating to possible mortgage fraud. The number of SARs reflects the number of people or organizations making suspicious loans, not the number of suspicious loans themselves. The reports are released quarterly, and we've organized them below by county. The figures show the number of SARs per million residents. Click a county to see its history of SAR rates, or to compare one county to another.

The data, which is limited to counties with populations exceeding 50,000 residents, comes from the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network, or FinCEN, a division of the Treasury Department, and runs from 2006 through the first quarter of this year.
